# Problem Statement

# Spotify AI Discovery Companion

## Background

Spotify has one of the world's most sophisticated music recommendation systems, powered by collaborative filtering, listening history, and audio intelligence. Despite these capabilities, a significant portion of listening still comes from repeat playlists, familiar artists, and previously discovered tracks.

As part of the Growth Team, the objective is to increase meaningful music discovery while reducing repetitive listening behavior.

To better understand this problem, two stages of research were conducted:

1. **AI-powered review analysis** across 700+ public reviews from the App Store, Play Store, Reddit, Spotify Community, and social media.
2. **Primary user research** through a survey of Spotify users to validate the findings.

---

# Target User

**Daily Spotify users aged 18–28** who frequently listen to music while studying, working, commuting, working out, or relaxing.

These users spend significant time on Spotify but often return to familiar playlists and artists instead of actively exploring new music.

---

# Research Findings

The review analysis and user research revealed several consistent patterns.

### Listening behavior is repetitive

A large majority of respondents reported that they often return to the same playlists, liked songs, or familiar artists instead of exploring new music.

---

### Music discovery happens outside Spotify

Many users discover songs through platforms such as Instagram Reels, YouTube Shorts, or social media before searching for them on Spotify.

Spotify is frequently used for playback rather than discovery.

---

### Recommendations lack contextual relevance

Users reported that recommendations often fail to reflect their current mood or activity.

Although Spotify understands long-term listening preferences, it struggles to understand what users want to listen to in the present moment.

---

### Users need confidence before trying unfamiliar music

Many respondents indicated they would be more willing to try new songs if they could quickly preview them before committing to the full track.

This suggests that the challenge is not only recommendation quality but also reducing the uncertainty associated with trying unfamiliar music.

---

# Root Cause

Spotify's recommendation system is highly effective at predicting music users have enjoyed historically.

However, it primarily relies on historical listening behavior and collaborative filtering.

It has limited understanding of a user's **current intent**, including:

* Current mood
* Current activity
* Immediate listening context

As a result, recommendations often optimize for familiarity rather than exploration, encouraging repetitive listening patterns.

---

# Problem Statement

**Daily Spotify users who want to discover new music often return to familiar playlists because Spotify's recommendations rely heavily on historical listening behavior and lack an understanding of the user's current intent. Without contextual recommendations and confidence-building experiences, users hesitate to explore unfamiliar music and increasingly rely on external platforms for discovery.**

---

# Why Solving This Matters

Improving music discovery benefits both users and Spotify.

For users, it creates a more personalized and engaging discovery experience while reducing repetitive listening behavior.

For Spotify, it increases meaningful discovery, encourages exploration of new artists, strengthens user engagement, and reduces dependence on external platforms as the primary source of music discovery.

---

# Product Opportunity

There is an opportunity to build an AI-native experience that complements Spotify's existing recommendation system.

Instead of replacing Spotify's recommendations, the solution should help users express their current intent, understand why recommendations are relevant, and confidently explore unfamiliar music through contextual explanations and playable previews.

The proposed solution is **Spotify AI Discovery Companion**—an optional AI-powered feature that acts as an intelligent reasoning layer between the user and Spotify's existing recommendation engine.

---

# Success Criteria

The solution should:

* Reduce repetitive listening behavior.
* Increase meaningful music discovery.
* Improve user confidence when trying unfamiliar songs.
* Keep users within Spotify for discovery instead of relying on external platforms.
* Demonstrate how AI enhances music discovery beyond traditional recommendation systems.
